Are you ready to find out about JAMB cut off mark for Polytechnic 2017/18? We bring you only the most verified data! Check the list of Nigerian Polytechnic tertiary institutions and their cut off marks!

Last week, JAMB cut off mark 2017/2018 for Nigerian higher institutions was fixed. As you know, The Joint Admission and Matriculation Board accepted the minimum JAMB cut off mark for tertiary institutions in Nigeria and made some other essential decisions concerning students’ enrollment.

These universities have also been allowed the power to increase the cut off mark for JAMB 2017 above the minimum according to their needs. Typically, the JAMB cut off mark 2017 depends on the status of university or college and training course.

Let's focus on Polytechnic institutions. You must have 100 points which is the the minimum JAMB cut off mark to enter Polytechnics in Nigeria. It should be mentioned that deadline for federal and state higher institution admission is the 15th of January, 2018. The private institutions must complete the enrollment of students by the 31st of January, 2018.

Nigerian Polytechnics have to make decisions concerning the first choice candidates’ admission by the 15th of October 2017, and the last day for candidates of the second choice is the 15th of December 2017. Then students will can now have the opportunity to choose any other University or Polytechnic until the January closing date.

In addition, the JAMB Registrar, Is-haq Oloyede reiterated that CAPS (the Central Admission Processing System) would be used for the regulation of the admission processes into higher institutions (polytechnics included). He mentioned that tertiary institutions could also adopt the dual-mode system.

To avoid illegal admissions, Mr. Is-haq Oloyede added that the process of admission will work automatically and candidates have to be confirmed by the JAMB Registrar.

Also, Mr. Adamu Adamu (the Minister of Education) announced a cancellation of the Post-UTME examination prohibition. He claimed that post-UTME prohibition led a lot of candidates and some tertiary institution to violate the rules.

Since the ban has been canceled, candidates now need to take an exam before admission. The fee for the exam should not be over 2000 Naira. Mr. Adamu Adamu also believed that a high percentage of candidates who participated in the 2017 UTME will get the chance to enter tertiary education institutions in Nigeria this academic year.
