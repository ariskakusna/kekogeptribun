Hugh Grant and Julia Roberts divorce in Notting Hill 2

August 06, 2024
post

Richard Curtis has revealed that he has written Notting Hill 2 and Hugh Grant and Julia Roberts would be getting divorced in it.

The writer of Four Weddings and a Funeral, Notting Hill and Love Actually made headlines this week for saying he felt bad about outdated lines in his films that he deemed no longer funny to a new generation.

Now, speaking on an episode of the Have You Seen? podcast, Curtis revealed: “Recently I wrote - I was going to do it for Comic Relief but it wasn’t good enough - I wrote Notting Hill 2.”

The 66-year-old added: “[It] was going to be a 10-minute special set in the divorce lawyer’s office with Hugh and Julia splitting up, and then they both realised they loved each other again.”

However, he admitted that making a sequel to a beloved romantic comedy was “a tricky road to go down”.

Curtis said: “I do think the second version of a romantic film is a tricky thing because what you do is you start with people who are together and you have to split them apart and then you have to put them back together again.”

Pressed on whether that is why he had never done a sequel to his beloved romantic comedies, he said: “Very much so, my daughter talks a lot about the fact that none of the people in any of my film’s marriages would have lasted longer than about three weeks.”

He added: “The problem with dramas about marriages is that it’s always literally got to be about either divorce or murder and yet the situation with most marriages is that they just go on and on and on.

“Actually the most realistic versions…of how marriage potters along, often I think happens in comedies.”

The screenwriter, producer and director also told the TV reviews podcast hosts that Love Actually was an “atrocious” film.

He said: “Love actually is just, be it a good or – as my daughter would say, an atrocious racist, sexist, fattest film – it’s in fact nine romantic comedies done in 10 minutes each.”

It comes after he has promised never to make “fat” jokes in his work again after being taken to task by his daughter.

A new generation of Love Actually viewers has been particularly appalled by the hit 2003 film’s depiction of Martine McCutcheon’s character.

She played Natalie, a tea lady at No10, whose father calls her “Plumpy” and an aide to the prime minister calls her “the chubby girl” with a “pretty sizeable a---”.

Speaking on stage at the Cheltenham Literature Festival, where he was interviewed by his 28-year-old daughter, Scarlett, he said: “I remember how shocked I was five years ago when Scarlett said to me, ‘You can never use the word fat again’.

“And, wow, you were right. In my generation, calling someone ‘chubby’ ... in Love, Actually, there are endless jokes about that. I think I was behind the curve and those jokes aren’t any longer funny.

“I don’t feel I was malicious at the time but I feel I was unobservant and not as clever as I should have been.”
