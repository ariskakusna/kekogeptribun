Doyle Bramhall II (born December 24, 1968) was reared in a family steeped with the blues and rock & roll sounds native to Texas, his home state. Doyle Bramhall, Sr., was a longtime collaborator with boyhood buddies Stevie Ray Vaughan and Jimmie Vaughan, and played drums for famed bluesmen Lightnin’ Hopkins and Freddie King. Doyle is one of the rare guitarists that performs with his guitar strung backwards. The instrument is strung for a right-handed performer, yet he plays left-handed. Because he bends the strings by drawing them downwards rather than pushing them upwards as is customary, his playing has a distinct sound. Other guitarists that employ this bending method include the late Canadian blues musician  Jeff Healey, who owing to blindness played with his guitar flat on his lap. Doyle travelled with Jimmie Vaughan’s band The Fabulous Thunderbirds when he was 18 years old. Two years later, he co-founded the blues rock band Arc Angels with fellow Austinite Charlie Sexton and Chris Layton and Tommy Shannon from Stevie Ray Vaughan’s rhythm section. Doyle got phone calls from both Roger Waters and Eric Clapton after the publication of Jellycream, Doyle’s second album and solo debut for RCA, in 199Doyle played lead guitar and sang background and lead vocals on Waters’ In the Flesh tour. His performances were captured on the “In the Flesh – Live” live CD and DVD that followed.
